Why I am Passionate about Keyboards

I type a lot, both for my job and in my free time. This can be anywhere from six to ten hours per day, which adds up very quickly. I never thought about keyboards much until recently when I fell down the rabbit hole of ergonomic keyboards. I had always thought developing RSI and Carpal Tunnel syndrome were an inevitable consequence of being a nerdy programmer, like how a chemical researcher must accept that their lungs will turn into a toxic mess, or a boxer knows that their brain will shrivel into a black lump.

Luckily we can avoid a future of painful wrists by bursting from the bubble of conventional keyboards!

Sins of Conventional Keyboards

Traditional keyboards make no sense in many ways. Some of the biggest blunders are:

  • Each row is horizontally staggered, which makes it awkward to reach many keys (especially the number row).
  • Your hands are forced to be very close together while typing, which is bad for both your wrists and shoulders.
  • Most keyboards let you tilt the back of the keyboard up. This is terrible for your wrists.
